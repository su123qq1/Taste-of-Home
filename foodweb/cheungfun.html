Cheung Fun

traditional Cantonese food

What is Cheung Fun

Cheung Fun is a kinds of food that is made of rice milk, meat and vegetable. It also has a name called steamed rice roll. There are two types of cheung fun. One is named drawer style cheung fun, the other one is called Beulah style cheung fun. They tastes similar, but have different looks. The length of the drawer style cheung fun is longer than the beuhlah style cheung fun. Therer are a lot of tastes as well, which depends on different kinds of food that is put inside the roll. Shrimp Cheung fun, beef cheung fun, and fried dough cheung fun are the most popular kinds.

How to make Cheung Fun?

Toss the shrimp with water, sugar and baking soda. After 2 hours, rinse the shrimp under cold water and dry it with paper towel. Marinate the shrimp with sesame oil, salt, cornstarch, and white pepper. Combine rice flour, wheat starch, cornstarch, and salt. Add water and stir for 3 minutes. Pre-boil water in steamer. Place the shrimp on a plate, and steam them for 2 minutes. Lift the soaking wet cotton cloth out of the water and line the bottom of the pan with it. With the water in the steamer boiling, use the plate gripper and lower the pan into the steamer. Cover the lid and steam on high for 2 minutes. Remove the pan from the steamer. Lift the cloth out of the pan, and place onto the oiled surface. Scrape the rice noodle sheet off the cotton cloth onto the oiled surface. Peel off the cotton cloth. Cut the rice noodle in half crosswise and line up four cooked shrimp. Roll the rice sheet to wrap up the shrimp. Cut each roll in half, and drizzle with the sauce.

Where Cheung Fun originated?

Cheung Fun origniated in Guangdong province during Tang Dynasty. It is one kind of Cantonese sepecialists. During the Qing Dynasty, Qianlong period, the Qianlong Emperor loved Cheung fun when he was traveling in Guangdong, and he gave the name of "cheung fun" to it. By the time, Cheung fun became more and more popular in Guangdong and more resteruant started making it.
